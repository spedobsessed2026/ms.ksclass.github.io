👩‍🏫 Meet Ms. Karabetsos

My Philosophy: I believe every child communicates. My role is to find the "how" and build a bridge between home and school so learning never stops.

With a focus on UDL, I ensure that our K-2 classroom is a sensory-regulated environment where students feel safe to take risks and grow.

🏫 Our Environment

Our classroom uses visual boundaries, individual workstations, and sensory "reset" zones. We partner with families to mirror these structures at home, ensuring consistency that reduces anxiety and boosts independence.

📚 What We Are Learning

🔢 Eureka Math

We are exploring Numbers to 10. We use "manipulatives" (things we can touch) to count.

Try this at home:

Count pieces of cereal during breakfast. Use the "touch-point" method we practiced!

🧩 Unique Learning System

Theme: Community Helpers. We are learning about doctors, mail carriers, and teachers.

Try this at home:

Point out the "Mail Truck" or "Fire Station" when driving in the community.

About the Teacher

Education: Graduate Student at National Louis University (Special Education & PEL).

Professional Philosophy

I believe that every behavior is communication. My goal is to empower students with the tools they need to navigate the world independently while fostering a deep partnership with the families who know them best.

Our Classroom Environment

Our room is designed using UDL principles to minimize sensory overload and maximize independence. We use Unique Learning System (ULS) for thematic ELA and Eureka Math, adapted with tactile manipulatives.

Structured Work Stations

Visual boundaries and clear "start/finish" tasks help students master independent work skills that transfer to home chores.

Communication Rich

From PECS to high-tech AAC devices, our environment ensures no voice goes unheard.
